### Authentic Roman Pasta Carbonara

Carbonara is a masterclass in Italian culinary magic: creating a rich, velvety sauce using just eggs, cheese, and pasta water—**no cream allowed!**

Here is how to make the real deal.

---

### Ingredients (Serves 4)

* **400g** Spaghetti or Rigatoni
* **150g** Guanciale (cured pork cheek) or Pancetta, cut into thick strips
* **4** Large Egg Yolks + **1** Whole Egg (the whole egg keeps the sauce from getting too dry)
* **75g** Pecorino Romano cheese, finely grated (plus extra for serving)
* **1 tbsp** Freshly cracked black pepper (coarse is best)
* Salt for the pasta water

---

### Step-by-Step Instructions

1. **Prep the Sauce Base:** In a medium bowl, whisk together the 4 egg yolks, 1 whole egg, and the finely grated Pecorino Romano. Season generously with a shower of freshly cracked black pepper. Mix until it forms a thick paste.
2. **Crisp the Pork:** Place the guanciale (or pancetta) in a large skillet over medium heat. You don't need to add oil; its own fat will render out. Cook until the fat is translucent and the edges are beautifully crisp (about 5–7 minutes). Turn off the heat, but **do not drain the fat**.
3. **Cook the Pasta:** Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Drop your pasta and cook it until it is *very* al dente (about 1–2 minutes less than the package instructions), as it will finish cooking in the pan.
4. **Combine Pasta and Pork:** Ladle out about a cup of pasta water and set it aside. Transfer the pasta directly from the pot into the skillet with the pork and rendered fat using tongs or a slotted spoon. Toss everything together for 30 seconds so the pasta gets coated in that savory flavor.
5. **The Critical Step:** Turn the heat **completely off** and move the skillet to a cool burner. Wait about 30 seconds so the pan cools down slightly (this prevents scrambled eggs). Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta.
6. **Emulsify:** Immediately start tossing and stirring vigorously. Splash in about 1/4 cup of the reserved hot pasta water. The residual heat from the pasta and the starch in the water will melt the cheese and cook the eggs into a smooth, glossy, creamy sauce. If it looks too thick, add a splash more pasta water.

---

### 💡 Pro-Tips for Perfection

> **The Golden Rule:** The skillet must *never* be over direct heat once the eggs are added. The residual heat of the pasta is more than enough to cook the eggs safely while keeping the sauce silky.
> **The Cheese:** If Pecorino Romano is too sharp or salty for your taste, you can do a 50/50 split of Pecorino and Parmigiano Reggiano.
> **Serve Immediately:** Carbonara waits for no one. Serve it straight out of the pan into warmed bowls, topped with a bit more cheese and another crack of black pepper.
