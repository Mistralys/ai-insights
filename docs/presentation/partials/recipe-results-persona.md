# Smoked Mackerel & Garden Savory Carbonara (*Carbonara di Sgombro Affumicato*)

**Cuisine:** Italo-Provençal Fusion
**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 mins | **Cook Time:** 15 mins
**Protein Profile:** Reduced Meat (Seafood & Egg-Based)
**Color Groups:** Green · Orange/Yellow · White/Tan/Brown

---

## Ingredients

### Pantry, Fresh & Canned/Refrigerated

* Dried spaghetti — 400g (organic preferred)
* Canned smoked mackerel fillets in olive oil — 3 tins (approx. 300g drained weight)
* High-quality eggs — 4 whole eggs + 2 egg yolks (organic preferred)
* Pecorino Romano cheese — 80g, finely grated
* Zucchinis — 2 medium (approx. 300g), sliced into thin half-moons (organic preferred)
* Shallots — 2 medium, finely diced
* Fresh ginger — 15g, peeled and finely grated
* Freshly cracked black pepper — 1 tsp

### From the Garden

* Winter or summer savory — 2 generous sprigs (leaves stripped and roughly chopped)
* Fresh thyme — 3 sprigs (leaves stripped)

### Equipment Used

* Gas cooking stove
* Fissler deep sauté pan (stainless steel)
* Multi-purpose mixer robot (for grating cheese and slicing zucchinis)

---

## Method

1. **Prep the Carbonara Base:** In a medium bowl, whisk together the 4 whole eggs, 2 egg yolks, and finely grated Pecorino Romano. Season with a few heavy cracks of black pepper. The mixture should form a thick, pale paste. Gently flake the drained smoked mackerel into bite-sized chunks and set aside.
2. **Cook the Pasta:** Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil on the gas cooking stove and salt it generously. Drop the spaghetti and cook until just shy of *al dente* (about 1–2 minutes less than package instructions), as it will finish cooking directly in the sauce.
3. **Sauté the Aromatics & Greens:** While the pasta cooks, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil (or the flavorful oil from the mackerel tin) in the Fissler deep sauté pan over medium heat. Add the diced shallots and grated ginger. Sauté for 2–3 minutes until the shallots turn translucent and fragrant. Stir in the zucchini half-moons, chopped garden savory, and thyme leaves. Cook for 4–5 minutes until the zucchinis are tender-crisp and lightly golden on the edges. Turn the heat to low, then fold in the flaked smoked mackerel just to warm it through.
4. **The Carbonara Emulsion:** Use tongs to transfer the spaghetti directly from the boiling water into the sauté pan with the zucchinis and mackerel, allowing a small amount of starchy pasta water to carry over. **Remove the pan completely from the gas flame** to ensure the eggs do not scramble.
5. **Glossy Finish:** Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the hot pasta. Immediately begin tossing vigorously with tongs while stirring. Pour in a small ladleful (approx. 60–80 ml) of the hot pasta cooking water. Keep tossing continuously for 1–2 minutes. The residual heat will melt the cheese and bind the eggs into a velvety, glossy sauce that clings beautifully to every strand of pasta.
6. **Serve:** Plate immediately in warmed bowls. Finish with an extra dusting of Pecorino Romano and a scattered pinch of fresh garden savory.

## Tinkerer's Notes

* **Fiber & Grain Boost:** Because this traditional emulsion technique yields a dish naturally lower in fiber, consider upgrading the grain base. Use your **small flour mill** and **Grilletta dough mixer** to craft fresh whole-grain spelt spaghetti. Alternatively, pair this rich meal with a side salad of shaved fennel, wild arugula, and radishes tossed in a sharp lemon vinaigrette to easily hit your daily fiber goals.
* **The Citrus Lift:** To elevate the ginger notes and cut through the rich, oily profile of the smoked mackerel, grate the zest of half an organic lemon directly into the egg and cheese mixture before tossing.
* **Herb Customization:** If savory is sparse in the garden, substitute it with a blend of fresh garden marjoram and a tiny, finely minced hint of rosemary to beautifully complement the smoky fish notes.
