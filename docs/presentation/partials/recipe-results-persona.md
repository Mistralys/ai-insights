# Rigatoni alla Carbonara di Zucchine

**Cuisine:** Roman (Lazio)
**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 15 mins | **Cook Time:** 15 mins
**Protein Profile:** Reduced Meat
**Color Groups:** Green · Orange/Yellow · White/Tan/Brown

## Ingredients

### Pantry, Fresh & Canned/Refrigerated

* Rigatoni or Spaghetti — 360 g (organic preferred)
* Guanciale or high-quality thick-cut pancetta — 100 g (sliced into small strips)
* Fresh Roman zucchini — 2 medium (approx. 300 g), sliced into thin rounds
* Fresh organic egg yolks — 4 large yolks + 1 whole egg
* Pecorino Romano PDO — 60 g (finely grated)
* Freshly cracked black pepper — to taste

### From the Garden

* Fresh marjoram or thyme — 2-3 sprigs (leaves stripped, approx. 1 tbsp)

### Equipment Used

* Gas cooking stove
* Fissler stainless steel frying pan

---

## Method

1. **Cook the Pasta:** Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil, salt it deliberately, and drop in the rigatoni. Cook until 2 minutes before *al dente*—the pasta will finish cooking directly in the sauce later.
2. **Render the Pork:** While the pasta cooks, place the guanciale strips into the cold Fissler stainless steel frying pan. Set it over medium-low heat on the gas stove. Let the fat render slowly until the pieces turn deeply golden and crisp (about 6–8 minutes). Use a slotted spoon to transfer the crispy meat to a small bowl, leaving the glorious liquid fat in the pan.
3. **Sauté the Zucchini:** Turn the gas heat up to medium. Toss the sliced zucchini and the garden marjoram or thyme leaves right into the rendered pork fat. Sauté until the zucchini edges turn golden-brown but still retain their vibrant green core (about 4–5 minutes). Turn off the heat.
4. **Build the Egg Paste:** In a medium bowl, whisk together the 4 egg yolks, the 1 whole egg, the grated Pecorino Romano, and a very generous amount of freshly cracked black pepper until it forms a thick, smooth paste.
5. **Combine:** Drain the pasta, making absolutely sure to reserve at least 200 mL of the starchy pasta cooking water. Toss the hot pasta directly into the frying pan with the zucchini, stirring for 30 seconds so the pasta absorbs the savory fat.
6. **The Emulsion Twist:** Let the pan cool off the heat for 1 minute (crucial to prevent scrambled eggs). Pour the egg and cheese paste over the pasta, then immediately pour in about 100 mL of the hot pasta water. Stir and toss vigorously off the heat. Watch the magic happen as the starch, fat, and eggs emulsify into a glossy, velvety coating. Stir the crispy guanciale back in, add a splash more pasta water if it looks too tight, and serve immediately.

## Tinkerer's Notes

* **The Green Garden Flip:** If you want to lean entirely into the garden bounty, swap out the marjoram for a handful of torn fresh mint leaves added at the very end. The mint cuts through the rich egg yolk and salty Pecorino beautifully.
* **Vegetarian Variation:** Omit the guanciale completely. Sauté the zucchini in 1.5 tablespoons of high-quality extra virgin olive oil instead, and add a tiny pinch of smoked paprika (*pimentón*) to the egg mixture to mimic that distinct, smoky depth.
* **Nutritional Balance Alert:** Because this classic Roman adaptation is naturally lower in fiber and focused on rich fats, ensure you pair this with a crisp, bitter green salad (like arugula or chicory) or a side of steamed artichokes to hit your daily fiber goals. This meal provides roughly 23 g of protein per person; balance the rest of your 100 g daily target during breakfast and lunch with protein-dense options like Greek yogurt or legumes.
